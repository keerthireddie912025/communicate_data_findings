Investigation Overview¶

The objective of this presentation is to identify the key factors that influenced passenger survival on the Titanic. The analysis focuses on understanding how variables such as passenger class, gender, age, and fare are associated with survival outcomes.

Key Insights¶

Most passengers traveled in Third Class. Female passengers had a higher survival rate than male passengers. First Class passengers generally paid higher fares. Higher fares were associated with higher survival rates. Passenger class, fare, and gender together provide strong insights into survival.

Dataset Overview and Executive Summary¶

The Titanic dataset contains information for 891 passengers and 15 variables.

The dataset includes demographic information, passenger class, fare, embarkation details, and survival status.

This presentation highlights the most important findings discovered during exploratory data analysis using a small number of polished visualizations.

The objective of this presentation is to identify the passenger characteristics that most influenced survival during the Titanic disaster and communicate the findings using clear explanatory visualizations.

Survival Rate by Gender¶

plt.figure(figsize=(7,5))

sns.barplot(
    data=df,
    x="sex",
    y="survived",
    estimator=np.mean,
    errorbar=None,
    palette=["steelblue","lightcoral"]
)

plt.title("Survival Rate by Gender", fontsize=14)
plt.xlabel("Gender")
plt.ylabel("Survival Rate")

for i, value in enumerate(df.groupby("sex")["survived"].mean()):
    plt.text(i, value + 0.02, f"{value:.2f}", ha="center", fontsize=11)

plt.ylim(0,1)

plt.show()

Observation¶

Female passengers had a much higher survival rate than male passengers. This suggests that gender was one of the strongest factors influencing survival during the Titanic disaster.

Final Conclusion¶

This analysis identified several important factors that influenced passenger survival on the Titanic.

• Female passengers had substantially higher survival rates than males.

• First Class passengers generally paid higher fares and experienced better survival outcomes.

• Higher ticket fares were positively associated with survival.

• Passenger age alone did not show a strong relationship with survival.

Overall, gender, passenger class and fare were the strongest predictors of survival in this dataset.
